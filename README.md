## 📌 Project Overview

This project examined clinical and demographic predictors of prostate cancer using a dataset of 346 patients. The objective was to identify factors associated with prostate cancer status and evaluate their predictive value through univariable and multivariable logistic regression modeling.

The analysis focused on established clinical indicators including PSA levels, digital rectal examination findings, capsular involvement, prostate volume, Gleason score, age at diagnosis, and race. Statistical analyses were conducted using SAS to identify significant predictors of prostate cancer and evaluate their clinical relevance.

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

* Compare clinical characteristics between prostate cancer and non-prostate cancer patients
* Identify significant risk factors associated with prostate cancer
* Evaluate predictor strength using logistic regression models
* Develop multivariable models for risk assessment and prediction
* Interpret odds ratios and confidence intervals for clinically relevant predictors

## 📈 Statistical Methods

### Descriptive Analysis

Continuous variables were summarized using:

* Mean
* Standard deviation
* Median
* Minimum
* Maximum

Categorical variables were summarized using:

* Counts
* Percentages

Group comparisons were conducted using:

* Student's t-tests
* Chi-square tests
* Fisher's exact tests where appropriate

### Univariable Logistic Regression

Separate logistic regression models were fitted for each predictor to estimate:

* Odds Ratios (OR)
* 95% Confidence Intervals
* P-values

### Multivariable Logistic Regression

Two multivariable models were developed:

**Model A**

* Included predictors significant in univariable analyses

**Model B**

* Included all candidate predictors

Adjusted Odds Ratios (AORs), 95% confidence intervals, and p-values were reported for each model.

## 🔍 Key Findings

### Significant Predictors

The strongest predictors of prostate cancer included:

* PSA level
* Digital Rectal Examination findings
* Gleason score category

### Digital Rectal Examination

Compared with patients without nodules:

* Right unilobar nodules were associated with substantially increased odds of prostate cancer
* Bilobar nodules demonstrated the strongest association with prostate cancer status

### Gleason Score

Higher Gleason score categories were strongly associated with prostate cancer:

* GS2 patients had significantly increased odds of prostate cancer
* GS3 patients demonstrated the highest risk estimates in multivariable models

### PSA

Higher log-transformed PSA levels remained a significant predictor after adjustment for other clinical variables.
